## R1 Federal Funding Dependency

Federal funding has accounted for the majority of research expenditure at U.S. R1 universities since the modern research enterprise took shape in the 1970s. The depth of that dependency is systematically examined across the full arc of the HERD survey record in this analysis, which draws on five decades of NSF Higher Education Research and Development (HERD) Survey data — spanning 1974 to 2024. I measured federal funding as a share of total R&D expenditure at R1 institutions at the national and state levels. A linear model documents a statistically significant long-run decline from approximately 75% federal share in the mid-1970s to 55% by 2024; an ARIMA(1,1,1) forecast projects continued stabilization near 55% through 2034 under historical trends, with a lower confidence bound near 47% — consistent with scenarios of sustained federal austerity. An animated choropleth map shows how dependency has shifted geographically over fifty years, enabling state- and district-level interpretation of exposure to the federal funding cuts currently underway.
